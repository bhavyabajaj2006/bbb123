4 Ways To Prepare For Term-1 Board Examination


In the year 2020, due to the pandemic class 10 as well as class 12 board examinations were canceled. So, this year Central Board of Education has divided the syllabus into two terms and hence, term wise examination would take place in the session 2021-2022.The change in the pattern and syllabus of class X resulted in high degree of anxiety among the students. Every student appearing for the boards this year is confused that how to study for the board examination . So today, I would share some tips so that you can study for the board examination-


1. Read the full chapter –

NCERT is very important for the understanding of the chapter so, You should first read the full chapter so that you know the whole concept and you can answer every question.


2. Practice objective type questions-

uestions again and again so that it would be easy for you to understand the type of questions that would come in the exam.


3. Read every question carefully-

All questions are important ,you should study all the questions carefully, whether it’s one marker or five marker it doesn’t matter .Don’t think that the exam is objective so you need not read the long questions.


4. Practice the sample paper released by CBSE-

On 3 September, 2021 CBSE has released sample paper on their official website, which can give you an understanding of how the questions would be asked in the exams.
You should solve those sample papers as it is very important

If the condition of the pandemic is fine, the Term-1 examination would be held in the school itself but if the condition is not so good then it would be conducted online. The examination will be conducted under supervision of the external centre superintendents and observers appointed by CBSE. The response of the students will be captured on OMR sheets which, after scanning may be directly uploaded at CBSE portal or alternatively may be evaluated and marks obtained will be uploaded by the school on the very same day. The final direction in this regard will be conveyed the to schools by the examination unit of the board. Marks of the Term I examination will contribute to the final overall score of students.
